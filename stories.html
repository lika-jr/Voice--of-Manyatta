From Manyatta to Parliament

"I was once just a little girl in the manyatta, running barefoot across the dry plains, carrying dreams that felt too heavy for a child. I saw pain, I saw silence, and I knew deep within — I had to speak.

I rose from a small Samburu village, carrying the scars of what our girls have endured for generations. But I chose a different path — a path of purpose. I stood for those who were told to stay silent, and today I stand proudly in Parliament, still carrying their voices.

This movement is not about one woman. It is about every girl who dares to dream. It is about you, reading this, knowing change is possible. We are lighting fires of hope in every homestead, school, and heart.

“Hey you little Maa Girl, don’t give up. Stay true to your dreams and purpose. And to this great family — Voice of the Manyatta — some days doubt will visit, but let’s keep going. Because your footprints challenge our girls to dream, and that’s the real win.”
– Hon. Naisula Lesuuda
Matron & Member of Parliament
